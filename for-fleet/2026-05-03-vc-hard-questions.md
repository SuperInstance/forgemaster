# VC Hard Questions
## Hermes-70B

Here are the 10 hardest questions I would ask the founders, along with the best possible answers and my ratings:

1. Q: How can you be sure your hardware constraints will cover all potential AI safety issues?
   A: Our constraints are based on the most comprehensive AI safety research to date. While no system is perfect, we believe our approach significantly reduces risk.
   Rating: Needs work

2. Q: Verifying a complex chip in Coq is extremely difficult. How can you be certain your formal proofs are sound?
   A: We've partnered with leading formal verification experts and are using state-of-the-art techniques. While challenges remain, we are highly confident in our proofs.
   Rating: Survives scrutiny

3. Q: DO-254 DAL A is a very high bar. Are you certain you can achieve it in the timeframe you've outlined?
   A: Based on our current progress and the simplicity of our design, we believe 6-9 months is achievable. We also have contingency plans if delays occur.
   Rating: Needs work

4. Q: Mask-locked weights seem inflexible. What if the AI model needs to be updated?
   A: Our chip is designed for edge inference, not training. Model updates would require a new mask, but this ensures the deployed model is immutable and secure.
   Rating: Survives scrutiny

5. Q: 28.8 TOPS/W is impressive, but how does it compare to the latest AI accelerators from large companies?
   A: While their raw performance may be higher, our Safe-TOPS/W metric accounts for safety. We believe this will be a key differentiator in safety-critical applications.
   Rating: Needs work

6. Q: Artix-7 is a relatively old FPGA. Why not use a more modern platform for your prototype?
   A: Artix-7 provides a good balance of performance and cost for our prototype. We'll move to an ASIC for production to unlock the full potential of our design.
   Rating: Survives scrutiny

7. Q: Four provisional patents seem low for a company claiming to have groundbreaking technology. Why is that?
   A: We've focused our patent applications on our core innovations. We plan to file additional patents as we continue to develop our technology.
   Rating: Needs work

8. Q: eVTOL and automotive are highly competitive markets. What makes you think you can break in?
   A: Safety is becoming an increasingly important differentiator in these markets. We believe our chip will provide a significant advantage in terms of safety and certification.
   Rating: Survives scrutiny

9. Q: $1.5M seems low for a test chip, especially at an advanced node like 22nm. How certain are you of your budget?
   A: We've gotten quotes from multiple foundries and believe $1.5M will be sufficient. However, we are prepared to raise additional funds if necessary.
   Rating: Needs work

10. Q: What happens if you run into unexpected technical challenges during development?
    A: We've assembled a world-class team with deep experience in chip design and AI safety. While challenges are inevitable, we are confident in our ability to overcome them.
    Rating: Survives scrutiny

Overall, while the company has some compelling technology, there are also significant risks and uncertainties. The founders' answers suggest they have thought through many of the key issues, but some areas (like DO-254 certification and patent strategy) seem underdeveloped. With additional work to address these concerns, the company could potentially be a promising investment, but it's not quite there yet.
