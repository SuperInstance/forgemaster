# Safe-TOPS/W Go-to-Market Strategy
## Hermes-70B

Here is a strategic go-to-market plan for establishing Safe-TOPS/W as the industry-standard benchmark for safety-certified AI accelerators:

1. Identify and engage key industry stakeholders and standardization bodies early in the process. Target organizations include SAE, ISO, RTCA, and IEEE. Build relationships with influential members and propose the creation of a new safety benchmark standard.

2. Aim for an 18-24 month timeline to get the benchmark standardized. This provides sufficient time for stakeholder input and revisions while maintaining momentum.

3. Publish the benchmark methodology as open source under a permissive license like Apache 2.0 or MIT. This allows transparency and builds trust. Require attribution to establish Cocapn/FLUX-LUCID as the originator.

4. Emphasize the critical importance of safety certification over raw performance. Position competitors' 0 Safe-TOPS/W scores as reflecting their lack of investment in functional safety, not an inherent disadvantage. 

5. Execute a multi-channel PR campaign including:
- Publish a detailed white paper explaining the benchmark methodology and its importance
- Present at key industry conferences and webinars 
- Engage in social media discussions around safety in AI hardware
- Secure press coverage and analyst reports validating the need for a safety benchmark

6. Partner with a respected certification body like TÜV SÜD or UL to provide third-party validation of Safe-TOPS/W scores and auditing of compliance.

7. Proactively address potential claims of bias by highlighting the benchmark's basis in industry safety standards, not proprietary architectures. Emphasize transparency and stakeholder input in the methodology.

8. Build robust anti-gaming provisions into the benchmark, such as requiring full transparency of the hardware and software environment, and third-party auditing. Regularly update the benchmark to close loopholes.

9. Educate customers on the economic and legal risks of deploying uncertified AI hardware. Emphasize the potential liability, certification delays, and reputational damage. Highlight Safe-TOPS/W as the key metric for assessing safety.

10. Timeline:
- Q1: Announce intent to create benchmark, engage stakeholders 
- Q2: Publish initial benchmark methodology as open source
- Q3: Refine methodology based on feedback, partner with certification body
- Q4: Submit benchmark for standardization, execute PR campaign
- Q1+18 months: Achieve formal standardization, require Safe-TOPS/W disclosure

The key is to frame Safe-TOPS/W as the industry's first safety-centric benchmark filling a critical gap, not an arbitrary metric favoring one architecture. Engage stakeholders early, build a strong economic case for customers, and maintain transparency to establish credibility and drive adoption.
