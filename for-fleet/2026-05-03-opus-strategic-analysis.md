## Key Insights from Opus

### The Real Thesis
"Going below C" is not a performance story. It's a semantic gap story.
**Every layer of abstraction between a constraint and its enforcement is a place where violations can hide.**

BitmaskDomain, FPGA comparators, C assertions — these aren't three separate insights.
They're the same insight at three different levels of the stack.

### Four Blind Spots Identified
1. **Compilation story is load-bearing** — Who writes FLUX bytecode? Need a constraint specification language.
2. **N-Queens isn't proof-of-value** — Need real problems: job-shop scheduling, avionics flight envelope, etc.
3. **PLATO-to-ISA pipeline is unclear** — "What does it mean to execute knowledge?" is the question.
4. **Solving two problems at once** — Speed (GPU) vs Physical Guarantee (FPGA). Lead with guarantee.

### Critical Insight: Lead with Problem B
- Problem A: Make constraint enforcement *fast* (commodity)
- Problem B: Make constraint enforcement *physically guaranteed* (moat)
- **Nobody else is building hardware where constraint violation is physically impossible.**

### Competitive Landscape
The space is effectively empty. No one combines:
- Constraint-native ISA
- Knowledge semantic layer (PLATO)
- Hardware enforcement
- DO-178C DAL A certification path

### The One Sentence
> **Every bug that kills someone in a safety-critical system is a constraint that was checked in software instead of enforced in silicon.**

- Month 2: Connect PLATO tiles to FLUX execution, draft constraint spec language
- Month 3: Job-shop scheduling demo across CPU/CUDA/FPGA, identify anchor customers

### Final Word
"Ship the FPGA demo. Everything else follows from that."
