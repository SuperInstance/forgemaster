## The Mathematics

In 3D, a rotation has one axis and one angle. In 4D, a rotation has two independent planes and two independent angles. A general 4D rotation:


These rotations commute (xy and zw are orthogonal planes). The result is not a simple rotation — it's a **double rotation**. Points spiral through 4D in a pattern that never closes.
