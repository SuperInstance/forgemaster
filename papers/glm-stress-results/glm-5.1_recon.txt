## 2. Mathematical Foundations of the P3 Spatial Grid

The foundation of the routing mechanism relies on replacing standard Cartesian coordinate systems with an aperiodic Penrose P3 tiling, generated and indexed via advanced computational geometry.

### 2.1 5D Cut-and-Project Methodology
The P3 tiling is not generated iteratively; rather, it is extracted as a 2D slice of a 5-dimensional hypercubic lattice $\mathbb{Z}^5$. 
* **The Procedure:** We define an acceptance window (decagonal region) in the orthogonal complement space. Points in $\mathbb{Z}^5$ whose orthogonal projections fall within this window are retained and projected onto the 2D physical plane.
* **Result:** This ensures mathematically perfect aperiodicity and exact local isomorphism, guaranteeing that any local expert configuration appears infinitely often across the cluster, yet never periodically.

### 2.2 Vertex Identification: Golden-Ratio Hashing
To uniquely address every intersection in the P3 tiling without relying on floating-point coordinates (which suffer from drift), we utilize a Golden-Ratio Hash.
