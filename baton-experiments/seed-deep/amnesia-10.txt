#### Session Breakdown (9:15 AM - 11:45 AM ET)
1.  **Formal Galois Unification Proofs Workstream (Lead: Dr. Elara Voss)**
    Walked through resolving a critical gap in the monoidal constraint closure lemma, which had blocked validation of cross-workstream constraint compatibility. The team identified a required dependency on an unpublished 2025 University of Amsterdam category theory preprint, and scheduled a sync with that research group for 2026-05-19 to align notation and proof scaffolding. All updated proof steps were pushed to the private GitHub repo `forge-master/constraint-theory-ecosystem/proofs`.
2.  **Telephone Game Fact Persistence Experiment Workstream (Lead: Mia Torres)**
    Presented pilot data showing 68% retention of verified factual claims when participants adhered to pre-defined constraint guards (e.g., no speculative embellishments, explicit fact-tagging) during narrative transmission, compared to a 32% retention rate in the unguarded control group. The team agreed to expand the pilot sample size from 50 to 200 participants, integrate the solver abstraction layer’s auto-tagging tool to streamline constraint adherence tracking, and share de-identified data with the open-source Computational Social Science Collective by 2026-06-01.
3.  **"Forgetting-as-Feature" Theoretical Paper Workstream (Lead: Prof. Raj Patel)**
    Shared draft Section 3, which formalized a framework for constraint-based "controlled forgetting" as a mechanism to reduce cognitive overhead in complex constraint systems. The team flagged a terminology mismatch: the paper used "constraint erosion" while the solver layer team referred to the same process as "constraint relaxation," and agreed to standardize terminology across all workstreams by end-of-day 2026-05-13. The group confirmed a target submission to *ACM Transactions on Programming Languages and Systems (TOPLAS)* for 2026-07-15, and assigned a graduate research assistant to compile citeable cross-workstream data for the manuscript’s methods section.
4.  **Unified Constraint Solver Abstraction Runtime Workstream (Lead: Jasper Cole)**
    Demoed a 48-hour MVP that could translate formal proof certificates from the Galois unification workstream into executable constraint checks, and ingest tagged fact persistence data to generate compliance reports. The team prioritized building cloud-native deployment pipelines for the runtime over launching a standalone benchmark toolkit, and assigned two senior software engineers to own pipeline build and testing.
