### 04:30–06:30 AKDT: Telephone Game Fact Persistence Experiment (Applied Constraint Validation)
This real-world simulation tested how core constraint facts persist across 8 human-to-human relay chains, mirroring emergency communication scenarios for the PNWMSC.
- **Experimental Parameters:** 14 curated core facts shared as the starting signal, tracked across 6 full relay rounds
- **Scenario Anchors (Critical Safety Facts):**
  1.  MV Epsilon drifted 200m east into Narrows Strait restricted shipping lanes
  2.  4,200 medical supply containers aboard MV Epsilon at risk of water damage
  3.  47,000 vessels sharing the same autopilot software vulnerability as MV Epsilon
- **Key Observations & Outcomes:**
  - Round 2 Recovery: A relay chain accidentally reintroduced a discarded initial fact: MV Epsilon’s hull number (IMO 9876543), which became a persistent cross-chain reference point
  - Crystallization Event: Rounds 3–4 saw rapid fact attrition, with only 6 facts surviving through all 6 relay rounds: the three core scenario anchors, IMO 9876543, the recovered hull number, and a spontaneous fictional character: Lila Marquez, invented by a first-round participant as a local harbor master coordinating the response
  - Narrative Survival Gap: Facts tied to concrete human-centric narratives (like Lila) had a 3.2x higher survival rate than dry technical or location-only facts
> The PNWMSC reps noted this data directly informs their emergency broadcast protocols, as it quantifies the value of embedding small, relatable narrative anchors to preserve critical safety information during multi-hop relay.
