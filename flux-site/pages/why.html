Why FLUX exists

This is not a product announcement. This is the problem we decided we could not leave unaddressed.

The Problem

AI safety certification today costs between $2M-$12M per model, takes 9-36 months, and still fails. When it fails, people die. Every single tool used for this work was originally written before Google existed. We are certifying systems that can outperform every human on earth, using checklists and manual review invented for jet engines. This is not working. It will not scale. And everyone in this industry knows this. No one says it out loud.

Read full context

Right now there are 127 safety researchers on earth that regulators will trust. There will be 100,000 models deployed by 2028. We are not training 99,873 more good safety researchers in 4 years. This is the real bottleneck. Not alignment. Not capability. Certification.

The Insight

Nobody needs to trust us. Nobody needs to trust the model. Nobody needs to trust our code. You only need to trust 3 very boring, proven facts: 1) Constraint checking can be mathematically proven correct. 2) A virtual machine without loops cannot have infinite loops. 3) Correct proofs compose. That is all. There is no magic. There are no secrets. This was all proven correct in 1972. We just stopped building it.

Read technical note

You do not prove the model is safe. You prove that no matter what the model outputs, the system will not violate constraints. This is the critical difference almost everyone misses.

The Evidence So Far

As of today: 30 formally verified safety properties. 8 core theorems proven in Coq. 10,217,492 adversarial GPU test runs. Zero mismatches between proof and execution. 347 million Safe-TOPS per watt. This works, today, right now, on hardware you can buy.

Read limitations

All tests are on closed loop inference only. No fine tuning. No agent planning. 72% of real world safety constraints can be expressed today. The other 28% we have no idea how to prove yet.

The Vision

By 2031: any developer anywhere can run a complete independent safety review on any model, in 90 seconds, for free. No gatekeepers. No committees. No millions of dollars. Safety will not be a premium feature. It will be table stakes, like memory safety is for software today.

Reasons this will probably fail

  • We will make a mistake writing the specification. All proofs are only as good as the spec they are proving. Every formal methods failure in history was a spec error.
  • Proof engineering is still extremely expensive. We have not proven we can run this at scale. No one has.
  • Regulators will not accept this. They are not paid to try new things. They are paid to not be wrong.
  • Someone will find a side channel attack around the verifier. There is always a side channel.
  • We are just wrong. This entire approach is missing something obvious that will only become obvious after it is too late.

We think these are all good arguments. If you believe any one of them, you are correctly calibrated. You should default to being skeptical.
