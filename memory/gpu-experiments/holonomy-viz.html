📖 What is Holonomy?

Holonomy measures the geometric defect when a point is transported around a closed loop on a manifold. In constraint theory, we send a query around a circular path (angle deltas summing to zero), snap to the nearest Pythagorean triple at each step, and measure how far the final position drifts from the start.

Zero displacement = flat manifold (snap is perfect). Non-zero displacement = curvature (the manifold's discrete structure introduces geometric drift). Higher resolution (more triples) → smaller gaps → less holonomy drift. This is the fundamental property that distinguishes constraint theory from continuous floating-point arithmetic: float drift is unbounded and random, constraint drift is bounded and geometrically predictable.
