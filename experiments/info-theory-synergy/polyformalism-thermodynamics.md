Let the lattice be a graph with vertices representing states and edges representing transitions. Define the partition function Z = ∑ₙ exp(-βEₙ) over energy levels Eₙ. The Boltzmann distribution pₙ = exp(-βEₙ)/Z gives probabilities of each state.

Phase transitions occur at temperatures where ∂²F/∂T² diverges, with F = -kT log Z the free energy. This happens when the system can access new regions of configuration space.

The entropy S = -k ∑ₙ pₙ log pₙ measures lattice disorder. A snap constrains the system, lowering entropy. More representations allow accessing more microstates, increasing entropy.

To optimize representations, consider the free energy F = E - TS. More representations lower E by fitting the landscape better, but increase S. The optimal point balances energy and entropy.

In summary, statistical mechanics predicts the existence of phase transitions, quantifies entropy changes upon snapping, and suggests an optimal tradeoff in the number of representations. The partition function Z and free energy F are key quantities.
