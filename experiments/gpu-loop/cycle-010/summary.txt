Cycle 10 (Seed-2.0-mini, fourth rotation) — 2026-05-17

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: Stress-tested the convergent theory (conservation = eigenvector stability × activation contractivity). PARTIALLY FALSIFIED.

THEORY REVISION: Conservation has TWO independent mechanisms, not one:
1. STRUCTURAL: rank-1 coupling (Hebbian SD) conserves trivially via algebraic identity — eigenvector rotation irrelevant
2. DYNAMICAL: full-rank coupling (Attention SD) conserves via eigenvector stability — rotation predicts CV

KEY FINDINGS:
- Hebbian SD: CV=0.0000 with 79° rotation at ALL scales (N=5,10,20,50) — direct counterexample
- r(rotation, CV) = 0.78 across mixed configs, but NON-LINEAR (R²=0.60)
- All 7 activations within 18% of each other under attention SD (CV 0.0033–0.0039)
- Activation contractivity is NOT a primary driver
- Eigenvector destabilization: non-monotonic (inverted-U) response
- Switching coupling: moderate rotation (6.8°) but moderate CV (0.030) — not well predicted

THEORY STATUS:
- ✅ Low rotation → good conservation (no counterexamples found)
- ❌ High rotation → poor conservation (Hebbian SD disproves at all scales)
- ❌ Activation contractivity matters (all activations ~equal)
- Need: effective rank of C(x) as the diagnostic that determines which mechanism applies
