ANALYSIS
======================================================================

1. Eisenstein Snap — Near-linear to 16 cores, efficiency drops at 24:
   - Near-perfect scaling (efficiency > 0.90) from 1 to 16 threads
   - At 24 threads, efficiency drops to 0.789 — hitting memory bandwidth wall
   - Compute-bound operation (FP rounding + comparison), limited by ALU throughput
   - Conclusion: Compute-bound, scales well with real cores, hyperthreading gives ~26% extra

2. 3-Tier Constraint Check — Two distinct regimes:
   a) LUT tier: Memory-bound, not compute-bound
      - Peaks at 12 threads, then saturates
      - Bandwidth limited: 666 Mops/s peak ≈ ~5.3 GB/s (8 byte key + overhead)
      - Efficiency drops to 0.146 at 24 threads — memory bandwidth is the wall
      - Conclusion: Memory-bound, NOT suitable for >8 threads without NUMA

   b) Linear+Full tier: Compute-bound, excellent scaling
      - Near-linear: efficiency > 0.90 all the way to 24 threads
      - 23.37× speedup at 24 threads (efficiency 0.974)
      - Each thread is compute-bound on its own chunk — no shared data
      - Conclusion: Perfect embarrassingly parallel workload

3. Holonomy Batch — BAD scaling, memory bottleneck dominant:
   - Erratic throughput: oscillates between 62-126 Mops/s
   - Efficiency drops to 0.065 at 24 threads
   - Root cause: each cycle is only 10 complex multiplies (160 bytes of data)
   - Work per cycle is tiny relative to memory traversal overhead
   - Threads are fighting over cache lines for the cycles[] and results[] arrays
   - 500K cycles × 8 bytes × 2 (re+im) × 10 elements = 80 MB — doesn't fit in L3
   - Conclusion: Communication-bound (memory pressure, not compute). 
     Needs larger per-thread chunks or NUMA-aware allocation.

4. Cyclotomic Field Projection — Good scaling to 8 cores, memory-bandwidth limited beyond:
   - Near-linear to 8 cores (efficiency 0.965-1.033)
   - Efficiency drops to 0.739 at 12 cores, 0.785 at 16, 0.552 at 24
   - 315 inner checks per point — compute-heavy, but data is read-once
   - 250K points × 8 bytes × 2 = 4 MB input — fits in L3 cache
   - But results[] is 250K × 16 bytes = 4 MB write — bandwidth contention
   - Conclusion: Compute-bound with a memory component. Scales well to 8 cores.

RECOMMENDATIONS
======================================================================
- Compute-bound operations (Eisenstein snap, Linear+Full constraint checks):
  Use full 24 threads. Near-linear scaling. No special allocator needed.

- Memory-bound operations (LUT, Cyclotomic projection):
  Use 8-12 threads for best efficiency. Beyond that, hyperthreads don't help.

- Holonomy batch: Needs restructuring. Too little work per cycle. 
  Batch cycles into larger chunks (500+ per thread) and use cache-line padding.
  Consider software prefetching.
