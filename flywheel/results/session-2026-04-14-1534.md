## GPU Experiment: Three Property Tests

### Test 1: Axis-aligned identity ✅ PASS
```
snap(1,0) and snap(0,1) error: avg=0.000000 max=0.000000
```
Axis vectors ARE fixed points. Zero error. CT snap is identity on axis-aligned inputs.

### Test 2: Commutativity with rotation ⚠️ NOT COMMUTATIVE
```
rotate-then-snap vs snap-then-rotate: avg=0.0010 max=95.6408
```
**CT snap does NOT commute with rotation.** Max divergence of 95.6 on a 200-unit range. This is important — it means CT snap and rotation are NOT interchangeable operations. You MUST snap AFTER rotation, not before.

**Implication for robotics:** Sensors measure rotated frames. You must snap in the measurement frame, not in a pre-rotated world frame. Getting the order wrong introduces massive error.

### Test 3: Distinct Pythagorean directions in 2D
```
Count: 2,780 directions (sides < 1000)
Bits needed: 11.4 (log2)
```
2,780 exact directions representable with integer sides < 1000. Need 11.4 bits to encode all of them. This is the actual information capacity of the CT manifold at this density — far more than the LLM estimated (865).
